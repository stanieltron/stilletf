Principles
Gold as a behavior, not decoration
Sona uses gold sparingly to mark commitments, ownership and confirmed value. It never appears on destructive or uncertain actions. Dark ink surfaces are reserved for data-heavy contexts like ledgers and order books.
  • Max one gold primary action per view.
  • Never place gold on error or warning states.
  • Pair gold with spacious ivory backgrounds for primary screens.
Type & rhythm
Spacing scale
Base unit is 4px, exposed as 6 / 12 / 20 / 32 / 48 tokens. Use larger steps for vertical rhythm and keep line-length between 60–80 characters.

Components
Interaction & motion
Motion exists only to confirm intent. Hover states lift slightly, clicks settle quickly. Avoid playful easing curves.
  • Hover lift: 1–2px translateY, 120ms.
  • Pressed: drop shadow reduces, background darkens by ~4%.
  • Use subtle opacity fades for loading; no spinners where possible.
Usage
Where this cheatsheet applies
This system applies to Sona dashboards, onboarding flows, settings, KYC/AML forms and transaction journeys. Marketing pages may expand the palette but must keep the same core gold & ink relationship.
